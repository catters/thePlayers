It's So Easy Being Green

By Anne C. Levy

Things are humming along just fine in Story Book Land.

Patrick Pixie is busy at his computer, sending the right characters to the right books, but then… it all changes in the blink of an eye. Horrible things begin turning up in the normally “very green” world… plastic bags are found blowing down the streets, unrecycled empty plastic water bottles are rolling around everywhere, plastic waxed drink boxes are found on the very doorstep of Patrick’s office… and then the ugly duckling gets his neck stuck in a plastic six pack holder.

After some “plastic surgery,” on Duckling, Thumbelina, Alice in Wonderland, The Emperor (with old clothes) and other fairy tale friends check the Knowitall and find out that the world on “the other side” has become so filled with junk and litter that it is now falling into the world of stories. As Chicken Licken feared… the sky is now really falling, and it is filled with trash and litter.

Then things go from bad to worse. The “It’s So Easy to Be Green” leaflet they leave in each book is found to also have become litter, so the characters must take drastic action… a story book strike. All around the world, children find that their beloved story books have gone blank. It will be up to one little boy to tell them the hard reality… no stories until they clean up the world.

An original play that teaches children the value of “reduce, reuse, recycle” to save Mother Earth.
