T'Was the Night Before Christmas...
by Anne Levy

Something goes awry as the “Reader” tries to tell the ages old Christmas story. First the mice were “stirring,” and they stole the stockings hung by the chimney with care. From then on, nothing goes right until finally, Santa can’t “dash away, dash away, dash away all” because he fell through the porch roof. An original spoof on the famous tale.

Scrooge Has Left the Building
by Pat Cook

It’s midnight on Christmas Eve, (or is it Christmas Eve Eve?) so Marley puts on his best chains and head off (as usual) with his Ghost Pals to scare the nightcap off of Scrooge - but wait - What is the address again? Who is this guy living in Scrooge’s house - or is it even Scrooge’s house? Did anyone check the GPS? It’s the old tale with a great comic twist.
