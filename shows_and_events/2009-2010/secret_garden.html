The Secret Garden
Archive links

Click to see the gallery

From the novel by Frances Hodgson Burnett
Adapted by Tim Kelly

The delightful story that you loved as a child comes to life on the Phoenix Players’ stage.

Mary Lennox, a spoiled child and difficult child, is sent to live at her uncle's estate in England after her parents pass away in India from the “cholera.” The estate turns out to be a gloomy place for a little girl. Her uncle is rarely home, and everyone keeps telling Mary that the cries she hears in the night are only her imagination.

Mary finds happiness in the garden with the help of Dickon, the gardener’s assistant, who has amazing talent for talking to animals and making things grow. Dickon helps Mary unlock a secret garden, withered from years of neglect, and soon it and Mary begin to bloom again.

Mary eventually discovers the reason for the crying she has heard every night, and by the end of the story her garden and her new outlook on life, help her to reunite a young son and his father and create a warm and happy family for herself.
