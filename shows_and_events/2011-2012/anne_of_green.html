By R.N. Sandberg

Based on the beloved novel by Lucy Maud Montgomery, Anne of Green Gables tells the story of Anne Shirley, an orphan girl who finds herself in Prince Edward Island at Green Gables, a farm owned by Marilla and Matthew Cuthbert.

The Cuthberts, brother and sister, decide to adopt a boy from an orphanage in Nova Scotia to help Matthew run their farm, but due to a misunderstanding, they send Anne instead. Marilla decides she must go back immediately, but Anne’s charming ways convince her to give the child a chance.

Anne, a unique child with a vivid imagination, would rather be called Cordelia, as it is more romantic than Anne and is forever “vexed” with what she sees as the “curse” of having red hair. Her imagination and constant conversation drive quiet and prim Marilla to distraction, although shy Matthew falls for Anne immediately, which Anne claims is because they are “kindred spirits.” Anne finds out that it can be difficult to be a stranger in a 1890's Canadian farm community, but she eventually wins the hearts of everyone, including Marilla, Diana Gil, who becomes Anne’s “bosom friend” and Gilbert Blythe, a mischief maker and her competition for smartest in the local school.

This wonderful stage adaptation will delight adults and children of all ages.
