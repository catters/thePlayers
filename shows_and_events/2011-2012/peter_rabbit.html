Play by Joseph Robinette

The charm of Beatrix Potter comes to life in this play that combines five of her delightful tales interwoven with the fascinating life of their creator, Beatrix Potter.

The tales of Peter Rabbit, Jemima Puddle-Duck, Mr Jeremy Fisher, Two Bad Mice, and The Tailor of Gloucester are all presented in this enchanting, enlightening entertainment for all ages.

The play moves back and forth from the fantasy worlds Beatrix created to the intriguing Victorian life story of the author herself who overcame her lonely, strict childhoodin which her writings and drawings were discouraged—to become one of the most widely-read children's author of all times.

We also meet Beatrix's governess, Annie (who narrates the play), and Annie's children for whom many of the stories were originally written.

Both entertaining and educational, this play is a must for any child—or adult—who has ever fallen under the magical spell of Peter Rabbit and his many friends.
