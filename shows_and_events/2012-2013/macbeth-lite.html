Adapted by Nancy Linehan Charles

This adaptation of Shakespeare's immortal play is presented in a way that makes it easily understood by anyone, child or adult alike.

Shakespeare's plot and brilliant words are, sprinkled liberally with comments and jokes by the narrators who get the audience involved in the story and the play itself.

Shakespeare's Scottish tragedy is told with all of the excitement of the original - with "murder most foul," "treacherous treason," "brainsickly" guilt, and of course ghosts and witches - but these are interspersed with such comic relief as coconut shells for the sound of horses hoofs, riders on imaginary steeds, and witty commentary on the characters, the action, and the words.

In the end, the audience learns that life in Shakespeare's world was no different than it is today - you've got to be careful what you wish for and who you hang out with - and listening to witches can only get you in trouble.

It's Macbeth Lite - all of the taste and none of the cholera!!!
