The House at Pooh Corner

Written by A.A. Milne and adapted for the stage by Bettye Knapp

Winnie The Pooh, Christopher Robin, Tigger, Piglet, Eeyore, and all their friends from the 100 acre wood are charmingly presented in this stage adaptation of A.A Milne's story.

When the play begins, everyone in the 100 acre wood is worried because Christopher Robin tells them at a "Mergency Meeting" that THEY (his parents) want to send him away to "Education."

A plan is made to run away to the South Pole (because the North Pole has crocodiles) and Christopher Robin brings bandanas so everyone can pack their thistles, "haycorns," skipping ropes, and most importantly chocolate bars for the "Explore."

Before they can begin though, Owl swoops in to warn them of incoming danger... a horrible creature with big fangs is coming toward them. It isn't long before Tigger bounces in and wreaks havoc with their "Explore" plans, as well as many of their belongings.

All turns out well though when Tigger is made part of the family and Christopher realizes that "Education" allows him to come back to his friends on holidays.
