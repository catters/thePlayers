The Trial of the Big Bad Wolf

Synopsis

The Trial of the Big Bad Wolf is one of those perfect children’s plays; it keeps children entranced with their favorite story book characters, while teaching them important life lessons, but at the same time appeals to the parents who will find its clever humor incredibly entertaining and fun.

It seems that the Three Pigs are out for revenge and they have taken their case to court. No it’s not Judge Judy, but it certainly is a “court of last resort,” as Judge Wise O.Al presides over a trial with more twists in it than Red Riding Hood’s ringlets. While the media reports are not quite “on the mark” (can you say sensationalized?), the Wolf gets a chance to tell his side.

  • What can you expect when you build your house of straw and sticks?
  • Who was it that threw the pepper and ragweed out the window?
  • Was the Wolf really employed by “Welcome Wagon”?

In the end, we find that everyone ends up being guilty of some transgression - yes even the judge.

It’s a lesson in critical thinking, looking at all sides of the story, not believing everything you hear, and how a half truth can be just as bad as a lie.

All in all, a show filled with fun and learning for children of all ages, with lessons we all need to learn and re-learn throughout our lives.

A play you’ll end up talking about with your children, long after you leave the theatre.
