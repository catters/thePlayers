Written by Anne C. Levy

Children performing Shakespeare as if they were fluent in Elizabethan?

Snow White and Sleeping Beauty in The Tragedy of Hamlet?

Yes all of this is true but only when the Phoenix Players Phire Phlies present the classic Shakespeare drama.

In an original play, written by the Anne Levy, the director of the youth acting troupe, the production becomes a comedy, but loses none of its original intent. Thus, it makes this show a great way to introduce children to the Shakespeare and it contains enough clever wit to make it fun for adults too. In fact, even Shakespeare aficionados will find something to like--as the minor character Horatio narrates the play and insists that he is meant to be the real hero, with some pretty compelling evidence.

If you liked last season's production of Macbeth Lite, you will love this presentation of Hamlet by the Phoenix Players youth acting troupe. All parts will be done by children and teens from age 6-15, with the role of Hamlet played by Phire Phlie veteran actress Ava Carter.

The play will be presented from November 15-24 on Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays and tickets will go on sale in early October.
